# Beginner Lesson Plans (8 Weeks)

## Week 1: Introductions & Daily Routine

### Week Objectives
- Master basic greetings and introductions
- Introduce present tense of "to be"
- Describe daily routine using simple present
- Build confidence with short questions and answers

---

### Lesson 1.1: Hello & Introductions

**Duration:** 60 minutes | **Level:** A1

#### Warmup (5 min)
- Greetings: "Hi," "Hello," "Good morning," "Good evening"
- Model natural greeting: "Hi, how are you?"

#### Core Content (30 min)

**Introduce Yourself Pattern:**
```
My name is [name].
I'm from [country].
I speak [languages].
I work as a [job].
```

**Practice:**
1. Teacher models full introduction
2. Student repeats after teacher
3. Student creates own introduction
4. Teacher asks follow-up questions

**Common Responses:**
- "Nice to meet you too"
- "Thanks for asking"
- "I'm doing well"

#### Speaking Practice (15 min)

**Exercise 1: Mirror & Repeat**
- Teacher: "Hi, I'm Sarah. Nice to meet you."
- Student: "Hi, I'm [name]. Nice to meet you."

**Exercise 2: Question & Answer**
- Teacher asks: "Where are you from?"
- Student responds: "I'm from [country]."

**Exercise 3: Swap Roles**
- Student asks: "What's your name?"
- Teacher responds

#### Homework (5 min)
- Practice introduction with 2 people
- Record 30-second video introducing yourself
- Write 3 introduction sentences

---

### Lesson 1.2: Daily Routine Conversation

**Duration:** 60 minutes | **Level:** A1

#### Warmup (5 min)
- Review: Previous lesson greetings
- New: Time vocabulary (morning, afternoon, evening)

#### Core Content (30 min)

**Simple Present Tense - Routine:**
```
I wake up at 7 AM.
I eat breakfast.
I go to work.
I finish work at 5 PM.
I go home.
I eat dinner.
I sleep at 10 PM.
```

**Pronunciation Practice:**
- "At" vs "eat" (clear vowel distinction)
- "Wake" vs "work" (different vowels)

#### Speaking Practice (15 min)

**Exercise 1: Guided Response**
- Teacher: "What time do you wake up?"
- Student: "I wake up at [time]."

**Exercise 2: Daily Timeline**
- Student arranges cards with daily activities in order
- Describes sequence aloud: "First, I wake up at 7..."

**Exercise 3: Question Practice**
- "What do you do in the morning?"
- "Do you eat breakfast?"
- "What time do you go to work?"

#### Homework (5 min)
- Write your daily routine (5-7 sentences)
- Practice speaking it aloud 3 times
- Prepare to share in next lesson

### Lesson 1.3: Question Forms & Short Answers

**Duration:** 60 minutes | **Level:** A1

#### Warmup (5 min)
- Review: Introductions + daily routine
- Introduction game: students pair up

#### Core Content (30 min)

**Yes/No Questions & Short Answers:**
```
Do you work? → Yes, I do. / No, I don't.
Does he work? → Yes, he does. / No, he doesn't.
Are you happy? → Yes, I am. / No, I'm not.
```

**Wh-Questions:**
```
What's your name? → My name is...
Where are you from? → I'm from...
When do you wake up? → I wake up at...
How are you? → I'm fine, thank you.
```

#### Speaking Practice (15 min)

**Exercise 1: Rapid Q&A**
- Teacher asks 5-10 yes/no questions rapidly
- Student answers with short form

**Exercise 2: Information Gap**
- Student sees picture without names/jobs
- Asks partner: "What's her name?" "Where is he from?"
- Partner answers from their card

**Exercise 3: Question Chains**
- Student asks: "Do you work?"
- Follow-up: "Where do you work?"
- Follow-up: "What time do you start?"

#### Homework (5 min)
- Write 5 yes/no questions
- Write 5 wh-questions
- Practice intonation (questions rise at end)

### Lesson 1.4: Conversation Confidence Drill

#### Warmup (5 min)

- **Goal:** 2-minute conversation at home
- Record video of yourself in a conversation
- Self-review: Did you greet? Ask questions? Answer clearly?

### Lesson 4.2: Introduction to Past Tense (Regular Verbs)

**Duration:** 60 minutes | **Level:** A1

#### Warmup (5 min)
- Travel vocabulary review

#### Core Content (35 min)

**Past Tense - Regular Verbs (Add -ed):**
```
Present         Past
work    →       worked
visit   →       visited
travel  →       traveled
enjoy   →       enjoyed
stay    →       stayed
```

**Pronunciation Rules:**
- "-ed" sounds like /t/ after: p, k, f, s (walked, stopped)
- "-ed" sounds like /d/ after: b, g, v, z, m, n, l, r (loved, stayed)
- "-ed" sounds like /ɪd/ after: t, d (waited, needed)

**Pattern:**
```
I visited Spain.
She traveled by train.
We stayed in a hotel.
They enjoyed the food.
```

**But Also:**
```
I visited Spain LAST MONTH / YESTERDAY / IN 2023 / A FEW WEEKS AGO
```

#### Speaking Practice (15 min)

**Exercise 1: Pronunciation of -ed**
- Teacher says verb
- Student says past tense + sounds out ending
- Focus on natural speed

**Exercise 2: Sentence Building**
- Given: subject + past verb + location
- Student creates: "My sister visited London."

**Exercise 3: Q&A With Past**
- "Did you visit Spain?" → "Yes, I visited Spain."
- "What did you do?" → "I stayed in hotels and visited museums."

#### Homework (10 min)
- Write 8-10 sentences in past tense

- Practice speaking clearly

### Lesson 5.1: Pronunciation - Problem Sounds

**Duration:** 60 minutes | **Level:** A1-A2

#### Common Mistakes (Adjust based on your students' native language):

**For Spanish Speakers:**
- /ʃ/ vs /tʃ/: "ship" vs "chip"
- /θ/: "think" (doesn't exist in Spanish)
- /ŋ/: "ng" ending

**For French Speakers:**
- Long vs short vowels: "bit" vs "beat"
- /w/: "we" (differs from French)

**For Mandarin Speakers:**
- /r/ vs /l/: "rice" vs "lice"
- Final consonants (Mandarin has few)

#### Core Content (35 min)

**3-Step Pronunciation Process:**

**STEP 1: Listen**
- Teacher says word slowly
- Student listens carefully
- Notice tongue/lip position

**STEP 2: Imitate**
- Teacher models again
- Student says exactly like teacher
- Exaggerate mouth movement

**STEP 3: Practice**
- Student says word 5-10 times
- Each time slightly faster/more natural

#### Speaking Practice (15 min)

**Exercise 1: Minimal Pairs** (words that differ by ONE sound)
```
"ship" - "chip"
"think" - "sink"
"rice" - "lice"
"wet" - "vet"
```

Student hears teacher say one, points to the word

**Exercise 2: Sentence Stress**
- Correct: "I WENT to the STORE" (beats on content words)
- Not: "I went TO the STORE" (even stress)

**Exercise 3: Linking Sounds**
- "Did you" → sounds like "didja"
- "Want to" → sounds like "wanna"
- "Going to" → sounds like "gonna"

#### Homework (10 min)
- Record yourself saying 10 problem words
- Practice 3x daily
- Notice improvement by next lesson

### Lesson 6.1: Linking Sounds & Natural Speech

**Duration:** 60 minutes | **Level:** A1-A2

#### Core Content (35 min)

**How Native Speakers Really Talk:**

Native speakers don't say words separately. They LINK them:

**Common Linking Patterns:**

**Pattern 1: Consonant + Vowel**
```
"Did you eat?" 
Sounds like: "Didja eat?" (the d links to the next vowel)

"Want some coffee?"
Sounds like: "Wanna som coffee?" (the t disappears)
```

**Pattern 2: Same Sounds Connect**
```
"That train" → "tharain" (both are t sounds)
"I like eating apples" → "I like-eating apples" (k-blend)
```

**Pattern 3: Casual Contractions**
```
Going to → gonna
Want to → wanna
Can't → cann't
Didn't you → didntcha
```

**Pattern 4: Stress & Unstress**
- Stressed (LOUD): content words (nouns, verbs, adjectives)
- Unstressed (quiet): small words (a, the, to, is)

**Example:**
```
Careful:
"I WANT to GO to the STORE"
(4 beats)

Natural:
"I wanna go the store"
(sounds faster, more natural)
```

#### Speaking Practice (20 min)

**Exercise 1: Listen & Imitate**
- Teacher says sentences with linking
- Student imitates exact pronunciation + speed

**Exercise 2: Transition from Careful to Fast**
- Say slowly: "Did-you-eat?"
- Repeat faster: "Didyaeat?"
- Keep getting faster until natural speed

**Exercise 3: Reading with Linking**
- Student reads dialogue aloud
- Teacher marks where sounds should link
- Student re-reads with proper linking

**Exercise 4: Conversation Speed**
- Short exchanges at normal speed
- Student must keep up naturally

#### Homework (5 min)
- Record 2-minute conversation
- Focus on natural linking, not perfect pronunciation
- Goal: Fluency > perfection

### Lesson 6.2: How to Interrupt & Ask for Clarification

**Duration:** 60 minutes | **Level:** A1-A2

#### Warmup (5 min)
- Linking sounds practice

#### Core Content (25 min)

**How to Interrupt Politely:**
```
"Excuse me, but..."
"Sorry, can I say something?"
"Actually, I think..."
"Just to clarify..."
"Wait, did you say...?"
```

**Asking for Clarification:**
```
"Can you say that again?"
"I didn't understand. Could you repeat it?"
"What does that mean?"
"Can you speak more slowly?"
"Sorry, what did you say?"
```

**When You Don't Know a Word:**
```
"How do you say... [in English]?"
"What's the English word for...?"
"I don't remember the word for..."
"What do you call this?"
```

**Real Conversation Example:**
```
A: "I installed new software on my computer"
B: "Sorry, what does 'installed' mean?"
A: "Oh, 'installed' means to put or set up."
B: "Ah, got it. So you set up new software. Thanks!"
```

#### Speaking Practice (25 min)

**Exercise 1: Interrupting**
- Teacher talks for 30 seconds
- Student interrupts politely at 3 points
- "Wait, what's that mean?"

**Exercise 2: Asking for Clarification**
- Teacher deliberately speaks:
  - Too fast
  - Unclearly
  - With new vocabulary
- Student asks for clarification in multiple ways

**Exercise 3: Conversation with Interruptions**
- Natural conversation
- Multiple interruptions by student
- Showing active listening

**Exercise 4: Problem Resolution**
- Conversation breaks down (misunderstanding)
- Student asks for clarification
- Conversation continues successfully

#### Homework (10 min)
- Record conversation with 3+ interruptions
- Show natural back-and-forth
- Practice key clarification phrases

**Duration:** 60 minutes | **Level:** A1-A2

**Use Transition Phrases:**
```
"By the way..."
"Speaking of which..."
"That reminds me..."
"Anyway..."
"So... [new topic]"
"Have you heard about...?"
```

**Example:**
```

```
A: "So in 2015, I also traveled to..."

## Troubleshooting Guide for Instructors

**"Student speaks too slowly"**
→ Practice linking sounds (Lesson 6.1)
→ Use "shadow speaking" (speak together at slow then normal speed)

**"Student repeats words they don't understand"**
→ Explicitly teach: "Ask me! Say 'What does that mean?'"
→ Practice clarification (Lesson 6.2)

**"Student only gives 1-word answers"**
→ Always ask follow-up: "And why?" "How was it?"
→ Model full sentences: "I did this BECAUSE..."
→ Praise full answers

**"Student freezes when asked unexpected questions"**
→ Do more spontaneous Q&A
→ Have "practice mistakes" - let them use circumlocution
→ Celebrate: "You solved that without knowing the word!"

**"Student seems bored"**
→ Increase variety (different scenarios, topics)
→ Let student choose topics
→ Add games: ranking, opinion-based discussions
→ Make it personal: ask about their family, interests, goals
