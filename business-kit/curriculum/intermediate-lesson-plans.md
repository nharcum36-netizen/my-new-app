### Week Objectives

### Lesson 7.1: Expressing Opinions Clearly

**Duration:** 60 minutes | **Level:** A2-B1

#### Warmup (5 min)
- Recap: Beginner fluency from Week 6
- "What's your favorite food and why?"

#### Core Content (30 min)

**Opinion Phrases (with varying strength):**

**MILD:**
- "I think..."
- "I believe..."
- "It seems to me..."
- "In my opinion..."

**STRONG:**
- "I strongly believe..."
- "I'm convinced that..."
- "Clearly..."
- "Obviously..."

**DISAGREEING POLITELY:**
- "I respectfully disagree."
- "I see your point, but..."
- "I understand, however..."
- "That's a fair point, but I think..."

**Giving Reasons:**
```
I like Spanish food BECAUSE it has great flavor.
I prefer coffee ALTHOUGH it's expensive.
I think languages are important SINCE they help communication.
```

**Balancing Pros & Cons:**
```
Coffee is delicious. However, it's bad for sleep.
Online learning is convenient. On the other hand, face-to-face is better for questions.
```

#### Speaking Practice (20 min)

**Exercise 1: Rapid Opinion Statements**
- Topic: "Is coffee good or bad?"
- Student makes 5-6 statements using different opinion phrases

**Exercise 2: Opinion with Reason**
- Student states opinion
- Teacher asks "Why?"
- Student explains with "because" or "since"

**Exercise 3: Agreeing & Disagreeing**
- Teacher states opinion: "Language learning is easy"
- Student agrees or disagrees with reason
- "I disagree because it requires daily practice"

**Exercise 4: Debate (Simple)**
- Topic: "Is living in a city better than living in the countryside?"
- Student takes position
- Gives 3 reasons
- Listens to other view
- Responds to counterargument

#### Homework (5 min)
- Write 5 opinions with reasons
- Practice expressing opinions aloud
- Prepare for next lesson

### Lesson 7.2: Professional Small Talk & Workplace Conversation

**Duration:** 60 minutes | **Level:** A2-B1

#### Warmup (5 min)
- Opinion practice from 7.1

#### Core Content (30 min)

**Professional Topics:**
```
Job responsibilities
Work challenges
Career goals
Industry trends
Professional news
```

**Professional Phrases:**
```
"I'm responsible for..."
"My role involves..."
"One of my biggest challenges is..."
"I would like to..."
"In my experience..."
"I've learned that..."
```

**Meeting Language:**
```
"Can we get started?"
"Moving on to the next point..."
"To summarize..."
"What are your thoughts?"
"Any other concerns?"
```

**Example Workplace Conversation:**
```
A: "Hi Sarah, how's the new project going?"
B: "It's challenging, actually. My responsibility is managing the timeline."
A: "That sounds demanding. What's the biggest challenge?"
B: "Coordinating between departments. But I've learned to communicate clearly."
A: "That's smart. What's your goal for the project?"
B: "To finish on time and within budget."
A: "Good luck! Let's catch up next week."
```

#### Speaking Practice (20 min)

**Exercise 1: Job Description**
- Student describes their job and role
- 2-3 minutes
- Include: responsibilities, challenges, goals

**Exercise 2: Problem-Solving Scenario**
```
You have a problem at work:
- Your boss wants you to finish project in 2 weeks
- You need 3 weeks
- What do you say?
```
- Student explains situation
- Proposes solution
- Remains professional + polite

**Exercise 3: Networking Conversation**
- Two professionals meet at conference
- Exchange roles: student asks about other person's work
- Finds common ground
- Exchanges contact info (figuratively)

**Exercise 4: Disagreement at Work**
- Boss suggests approach student disagrees with
- Student respectfully explains alternative idea
- Uses professional language
- Remains calm + professional

#### Homework (5 min)
- Write 5 professional email topics
- Practice workplace vocabulary
- Prepare career story (2-3 min)

### Lesson 7.3: Meeting & Presentation Skills

**Duration:** 60 minutes | **Level:** A2-B1

#### Warmup (5 min)
- Workplace scenario review from 7.2

#### Core Content (30 min)

**Meeting Phrases:**
```
"Let's start with..."
"Does anyone have questions?"
"Moving on to..."
"Let me clarify..."
"To summarize..."
"Next steps are..."
"Meeting adjourned."
```

**Presenting Information:**
```
"Today I'll talk about..."
"There are three main points..."
"First, ... Second, ... Finally, ..."
"This is important because..."
"In conclusion..."
"Are there any questions?"
```

**Handling Questions in Meetings:**
```
Q: "I don't understand that point."
A: "Let me rephrase. What I mean is..."

Q: "That's too expensive."
A: "I understand the concern. However, the benefit is..."

Q: "When can we implement this?"
A: "We can start in two weeks, assuming we have approval."
```

**Mini-Presentation Structure:**
```
1. INTRODUCTION (30 seconds)
   "Today I'm presenting about [topic]"
   "I'll cover three main points"

2. BODY (2-3 minutes)
   Point 1 + example/reason
   Point 2 + example/reason
   Point 3 + example/reason

3. CONCLUSION (30 seconds)
   "In summary, the key points are..."
   "Questions?"
```

#### Speaking Practice (20 min)

**Exercise 1: Mini-Presentation**
- Topic: Hobby, skill, or work project
- Structure: Intro (30 sec) + 3 points (1 min) + conclusion (30 sec)
- Total: ~3 minutes
- Teacher takes notes

**Exercise 2: Q&A After Presentation**
- Teacher asks 4-5 questions about presentation
- Student answers, clarifies, adds details

**Exercise 3: Group Presentation Skills**
- Student presents simple info (3 min)
- Two other people ask questions
- Student handles challenging questions

**Exercise 4: Presentation Refinement**
- Student re-presents same topic
- Better flow, clearer details
- More confident delivery

#### Homework (5 min)
- Prepare 3-minute presentation on topic of choice
- Practice with clear structure
- Record and self-evaluate
- Plan: "What would make this better?"

### Lesson 8.1: Small Talk Mastery

**Duration:** 60 minutes | **Level:** A2-B1

#### Warmup (5 min)
- Week 7 presentation review

#### Core Content (30 min)

**Small Talk Scenarios & Language:**

**SCENARIO 1: Elevator/Casual Hallway**
```
A: "Hey! How are you?"
B: "Pretty good, busy week though."
A: "Tell me about it. Anything fun planned this weekend?"
B: "Actually yes, I'm going hiking."
A: "That sounds nice! Well, have a good one."
B: "You too!"
---Time: 20 seconds---
```

**SCENARIO 2: Coffee Shop (Longer)**
```
A: "Hi! How have you been?"
B: "Good! Busy with work. How about you?"
A: "Same. Did you see [TV show/movie/news]?"
B: "No, I haven't. Is it good?"
A: "Yeah, I really enjoyed it. You should check it out."
B: "I will! Oh, and how's your family?"
A: "They're great. My daughter just finished school."
B: "That's wonderful! Mine are doing well too."
---Time: 2-3 minutes---
```

**SCENARIO 3: Professional Event**
```
A: "I don't think we've met. I'm Tom."
B: "Nice to meet you. I'm Maria. Are you in the industry?"
A: "Yes, I work in marketing. What about you?"
B: "I'm in software development. What company?"
A: "I work for Microsoft. How about you?"
B: "Google. What's your role?"
A: "I manage campaigns. Interesting stuff. What do you do there?"
B: "I develop infrastructure. It's complex, but I love the challenge."
A: "Nice! Let's exchange contact info."
---Time: 3-4 minutes---
```

#### Speaking Practice (20 min)

**Exercise 1: Rapid Small Talk Exchanges**
- Teacher initiates casual small talk
- Student responds naturally
- 8-10 exchanges, varying lengths
- Each exchange gets more detailed

**Exercise 2: Sustaining Small Talk**
- Teacher asks opening question
- Student keeps conversation going
- Asks follow-up questions
- Changes topics naturally
- Extends to 3-5 minutes

**Exercise 3: Small Talk in Different Contexts**
- Casual (friend at coffee shop)
- Professional (colleague at work)
- Neutral (stranger on train)
- Each requires slightly different tone

**Exercise 4: Self-Initiated Small Talk**
- Student starts conversation with teacher
- Takes lead for 3-4 minutes
- Shows interest in other person

#### Homework (5 min)
- Practice small talk with native speaker (if possible)
- Or practice with yourself/recording
- 5 different scenarios
- Goal: Natural, flowing conversation

### Lesson 8.2: Advanced Vocabulary & Nuanced Meanings

**Duration:** 60 minutes | **Level:** A2-B1

#### Warmup (5 min)
- Small talk practice from 8.1

#### Core Content (30 min)

**Synonyms with Different Connotations:**

**WORD: "Interesting"**
- "That's interesting" = maybe good, maybe not
- "That's fascinating" = definitely good
- "That's curious" = unusual, maybe suspicious
- "That's intriguing" = mysterious, wants to know more

**PRACTICE:**
- Your friend: "I got a new job"
- You answer: "That's great!" (positive expectation)
- But if they seem uncertain, say: "How do you feel about that?" (open)
- If it's unusual say: "That's interesting! Tell me more." (curious)

**WORD: "Happy"**
- "I'm happy" = general contentment
- "I'm delighted" = very pleased, surprised in good way
- "I'm thrilled" = excited, enthusiastic
- "I'm satisfied" = got what I wanted
- "I'm relieved" = stress gone

**Vocabulary for Opinions:**

**WEAK AGREEMENT:**
- "That's a point."
- "I see your perspective."
- "That makes sense."

**STRONG AGREEMENT:**
- "Absolutely."
- "I couldn't agree more."
- "Exactly my thoughts."
- "You're completely right."

**PARTIAL DISAGREEMENT:**
- "You have a point, but..."
- "I see where you're coming from, however..."
- "That's valid, though I'd add..."

**WORD FAMILIES:** (related words, different forms)
```
Economy → Economic → Economist → Economical
Create → Creator → Creation → Creative
Believe → Belief → Believer → Believable
```

#### Speaking Practice (20 min)

**Exercise 1: Context-Based Word Choice**
- Scenario given
- Teacher: "What word would you use?"
- Student picks best word + explains why
- Practices saying it naturally

**Exercise 2: Nuanced Responses**
- Teacher shares news
- Student responds with correct word
- "Got the job" → "That's fantastic! Congratulations!"
- "Boyfriend left" → "I'm really sorry. That must be difficult."
- "Trying new hobby" → "That's interesting! How is it?"

**Exercise 3: Opinion Phrase Variation**
- Same opinion, 5 different ways to say it
- "I think coffee is good"
- "In my view, coffee has merit."
- "Personally, I'm a coffee advocate."
- "I'd argue that coffee is essential."
- "Coffee is, without question, beneficial."

**Exercise 4: Conversation with Vocabulary Variety**
- 5-minute conversation
- Student demonstrates range of vocabulary
- Shows depth of understanding

#### Homework (5 min)
- Study word families (10 words minimum)
- Use each word in a sentence
- Record 2-minute response about opinion
- Use varied vocabulary + nuanced expressions

### Lesson 8.3: Handling Complex Conversations

**Duration:** 60 minutes | **Level:** A2-B1

#### Warmup (5 min)
- Vocabulary practice from 8.2

#### Core Content (30 min)

**When You Don't Know a Word:**
```
NOT: "I don't know this word."
Better: "What I'm trying to say is... [describe without the word]"
Better: "There's a word for this, but I can't remember it. It's like..."
Best: "Is it... let me think... [attempts] or something similar?"
```

**Misunderstandings - How to Clarify:**
```
A: "I'm fed up with my job."
B: "So you're tired?"
A: "Well, more than tired. I mean I'm frustrated. It's not a good situation right now."
B: "I understand. What's the main issue?"
```

**Discussing Complex Topics:**
```
Topic: Politics, Religion, Controversial Issues
Safe phrases:
- "I understand the argument..."
- "Some people believe..."
- "There's evidence for and against..."
- "It's complicated because..."
- "I have my perspective, but I respect yours."
```

**Handling Emotional Topics:**
```
A: "I just found out I'm pregnant."
B: "Oh wow! How are you feeling?"
A: "Excited but nervous."
B: "That's natural. Do you want to talk about it?"
A: "Yes, I'm worried about..."
B: "Those are valid concerns. Have you thought about...?"
```

**Disagreeing Without Conflict:**
```
NOT: "You're wrong."
Better: "I see what you mean, and I respect that, but I experience it differently."

NOT: "That's stupid."
Better: "I understand that perspective. My experience has been different."

NOT: "You don't get it."
Better: "I don't think I explained that well. What I meant was..."
```

#### Speaking Practice (20 min)

**Exercise 1: Circumlocution (Talking Around Unknown Words)**
- Teacher asks: "What's 'procrastinate'?"
- Student doesn't use the word
- Student: "It's when you delay doing something. You wait until the last minute even though you know you should start earlier."
- Teacher: "Yes! That's procrastination."

**Exercise 2: Clarifying Misunderstandings**
- Teacher intentionally misunderstands
- Student clarifies gently + patiently
- Student: "Not exactly. What I meant was..."

**Exercise 3: Emotional/Complex Conversation**
- Topics: Relationship issue, health concern, career doubt
- Student discusses sensitively
- Shows empathy + understanding
- Asks supportive questions

**Exercise 4: Navigate Uncomfortable Topic**
- Controversial topic introduced
- Student disagrees respectfully
- Shows understanding of other view
- Maintains conversation without conflict

#### Homework (5 min)
- Record conversation discussing:
  - Something you disagree about
  - Respectfully showing your perspective
  - Asking about their view
- 3-4 minutes
- Focus on tone: calm, respectful, engaged

## Intermediate Level Notes for Instructors

**Key Teaching Points:**

**Shifting from Form to Function:**
- Don't correct every grammar error
- Focus on communication success
- Gentle corrections only on repeated patterns

**Building Confidence:**
- Praise attempt to communicate, not just perfection
- "You tried circumlocution! That's advanced!"
- Celebrate risk-taking

**Topic Expansion:**
- Let students choose discussion topics
- Build from their interests
- Relevance = engagement

**Natural Mistakes Are Learning:**
- When student mixes up words, use it as teaching moment
- "Interesting! You said X, but Y is the word for..."
- Normalize mistakes as part of learning

**Preparing for Advanced:**
- Introduce longer discourse (3-5 minutes minimum)
- Add complexity: multiple reasons, nuance
- Challenge with unexpected questions
- Move toward topic mastery (can speak on one topic 10+ minutes)
